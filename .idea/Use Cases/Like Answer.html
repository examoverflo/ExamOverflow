Like answer Primary actor: Student Goal in context: The student wishes to express their approval of an answer posted to the ExamOverflow by a fellow contributor. This answer has most likely been most useful for the student posting the like. Scope: Educational â€“ The overall likes mechanism which orders answers by their popularity as provided by the community of contributing students. Level: Summary Stakeholders and Interests Community: Group of students who contribute to ExamOverflow Students: Wish to contribute answers for their fellow students. The also like the answers provided. This is to give each answer a weighting, the higher this weighting (number of likes) the more accurate and/or helpful the answer is perceived to be. Administrator: Ensures a role of quality control. They have the power if they feel necessary to remove any misleading or incorrect answers as well as removing users where necessary. Preconditions: The answer must be posted before it can be liked. Each answer can only be liked by the same user once. Minimal Guarantees: An answer receives some likes verifies that the content of the answer is accurate and helpful. Success Guarantees: An answer that receives a high number of likes will be highly rated and thus should be a trusted source of information for the rest of the community. Trigger: Student likes an answer Main Success Scenario: 1. Student: Posts a question in order to receive relevant help in the form of textual answers or pointers or a video link to a tutorial from other fellow students in the community. 2. Student: Reviews all answers that the community has provided in relation to the posted question. 3. Student: Having reviewed all of the answers the student may decide to decide to like the answer they feel has been the most helpful or accurate. This will help fellow users who read the answers in the future as they can quickly seek out the most liked answers. Extensions: 3a. Student feels that the answers are not relevant and does not like any answer. 3b. Administrator reviews the question as well as the answers provided and decides that the forum is not being used for its purpose and thus deletes the question form the forum. This involves deleting all associated answers posted as well as all of the likes. Any likes that were contributed to the answer will now be undone. 3c. The user attempts to like an answer that they have already liked. Only one like per answer will be recorded and thus an appropriate error message will be displayed to the screen. 3d. The user decides to like an answer that they have previously disliked. This action will undo the dislike and thus increment the likes of the answer as per usual.
